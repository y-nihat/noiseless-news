## What happened

Cohere and the University of Toronto said on 16 July 2026 that they have
entered a multi-year partnership to support U of T's adoption of AI across
research, teaching, student services, and administration [1][2]. Under the
deal, Cohere's privately deployable agentic AI platform, North, will act as
the orchestration layer within U of T's enterprise AI platform — which both
parties describe as still forthcoming — coordinating workflows and access to
university information while keeping data under U of T's control [1][2].
North will also support U of T's AI Kitchen, a secure environment for
evaluating AI tools that the university says remains in a pre-launch phase
[1][2].

U of T chief information officer Donna Kidwell called the announcement "both
a homecoming and the beginning of a new stage of evolution," a reference to
Cohere's founding in 2019 by then-U of T students Aidan Gomez, Nick Frosst,
and Ivan Zhang [2]. Neither party disclosed a dollar figure or a specific
contract length; "multi-year" is the term both organizations used [1][2].

## Why it matters

The deal gives Cohere — the Toronto-founded, self-styled "sovereign AI"
company — a flagship university customer as vendors compete for enterprise
AI deployments inside Canadian research and public institutions. It is a
joint announcement by the two parties rather than independently reported
news, and the enterprise AI platform and AI Kitchen it will feed into are
both still being built, not yet live [1][2].
