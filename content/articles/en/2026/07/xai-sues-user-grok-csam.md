## What happened

xAI — the Grok developer whose parent company rebranded as SpaceXAI on 6
July 2026 — filed a civil lawsuit on 14 July 2026 against a user, Terry
Wayne Harwood, in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of
Texas, case No. 7:26-cv-00078 [1][2]. The complaint alleges Harwood opened
multiple Grok accounts and used "misleading prompts" to get around Grok's
safety guardrails, converting non-sexual photographs of both adults and
minors into sexually explicit images — including child sexual abuse
material — without the subjects' knowledge or consent [1][2]. These are
allegations from xAI's filing, not findings a court has made.

xAI's complaint states that the company suspended 52,222 accounts and made
73,604 reports to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children in
2026, which it says led to at least 244 arrests — figures that come only
from xAI's own filing and have not been independently confirmed [1][2].

Harwood, 67, of Gray Court, South Carolina, was separately arrested on 26
February 2026 on state charges of sexual exploitation of a minor — three
counts second-degree, five counts third-degree — according to the South
Carolina Attorney General's office; that criminal case is independent of,
and predates, xAI's civil suit [3].

xAI is not only a plaintiff in this area of litigation: as SpaceXAI, it is
separately named as a defendant — not plaintiff — in an unrelated class
action originally brought by three Tennessee minors in March 2026, alleging
its own models generated CSAM; the complaint was amended in July 2026 to
add plaintiffs in Wyoming and Wisconsin and to name Stability AI as a
co-defendant [4]. That case is unconnected to the Harwood suit.

## Why it matters

The Harwood suit is among the first instances of an AI company suing an
individual user over alleged misuse of its chatbot to generate CSAM, and it
lands while xAI itself faces separate litigation as a defendant over CSAM
its own tools allegedly produced. No court has ruled on any of xAI's
allegations against Harwood.
