## What happened

Meta announced Muse Image and Muse Video, its newest AI image and video generators, on 7 July 2026 [1]. Muse Image is available now in the Meta AI app, on meta.ai, in Instagram Stories in the US, and on WhatsApp in a limited set of countries, with Facebook access coming soon [1].

Independent reporting from TechCrunch and Wired describes a specific mechanic: any user can @-mention a public Instagram or Facebook account inside a Muse Image prompt, and the model will incorporate that account's photos into the newly generated image [2][3]. This is on by default — public accounts are included automatically, with no opt-in required — and Meta does not notify a person when someone else generates an AI image using their tagged photos [2][3]. Private accounts and accounts belonging to people under 18 are excluded automatically [2][3].

To stop their content from being used this way, a user has to go into their Instagram profile settings, open "Sharing and reuse," and turn off "Allow people to use your content on Instagram with AI features on Meta" separately for Posts and for Reels — a control Meta also documents on its own help pages [2][3][4][5].

The opt-out (rather than opt-in) design has drawn criticism from within the entertainment industry: talent agency CAA and the actors' union SAG-AFTRA have both publicly objected to Meta's approach, arguing that a person's likeness should require affirmative consent before being used to generate new images, rather than requiring people to actively turn the feature off [2].

## Why it matters

Because the feature is on by default and gives no notification, any public Instagram user's photos can be used by someone else to generate new AI images of them without that person's knowledge or active consent — unless they've already found and disabled the setting. That default is now the specific point of the industry pushback from CAA and SAG-AFTRA, and it puts Meta's design choice at odds with a growing expectation, pushed by talent representatives, that AI image tools should ask before using someone's likeness rather than after.

## Update, 11 July 2026

Meta has removed the Muse Image @-mention feature from Instagram entirely, three days after its 7 July launch, following the criticism from CAA, SAG-AFTRA, and users over its opt-out consent design [6][7]. A Meta spokesperson said the company had "heard the feedback that this feature missed the mark, so it's no longer available" [6][7]. Meta has not said whether a revised, opt-in version is planned.
