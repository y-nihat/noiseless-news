## What happened

Samsung is in talks to take an equity stake in French AI lab Mistral AI as part of its Series D funding round [1][2]. The talks are confirmed by two separately sourced reports: the Financial Times, citing people familiar with the matter, and Sifted, citing its own source with direct knowledge of the discussions [1][2]. Samsung has previously backed Mistral through its venture arm.

The Financial Times reports the stake could be worth roughly €1 billion, as part of a broader round that would value Mistral at around €20 billion — but these specific figures have not been independently corroborated by any other outlet; other reporting on the story, including wire coverage, explicitly attributes the numbers to the Financial Times without confirming them separately [1]. Samsung and Mistral both declined to comment when asked [1][2].

Mistral's most recent confirmed funding was a €1.7 billion round in September 2025; the company has not made any official announcement about Samsung's involvement or a new valuation.

## Why it matters

If completed, the investment would deepen Samsung's ties to one of Europe's most prominent AI labs as it tries to position itself as an alternative to US-based frontier developers. The reported €20 billion valuation, if accurate, would roughly double Mistral's valuation from a year earlier — but that figure rests on a single source for now, and independent confirmation is not yet available.
