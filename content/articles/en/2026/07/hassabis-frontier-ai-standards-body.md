## What happened

Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis published a proposal on 14 July 2026, titled "A Framework for Frontier AI and the Dawning of a New Age," calling for a new independent body to test frontier AI models before release [1][2][3]. The body would be modeled on FINRA, the industry-funded regulator that polices Wall Street under SEC oversight: frontier labs would initially join voluntarily, sharing models up to 30 days ahead of launch for testing that probes dangerous cyber, biological, and "deception" capabilities [1][2]. Hassabis writes that once that voluntary regime proves "effective and robust," formalizing it into a requirement — models would have to pass before U.S. market deployment — "could quickly follow" [1][2].

He envisions a majority-independent board that includes Turing Award winners and other credentialed experts alongside industry, government, and open-source representatives, with rules applying to all frontier-class models "no matter their country of origin or whether they are open or closed" [1][2]. Hassabis says he wants the body operational before the end of 2026 [1][3].

## Why it matters

This is a proposal from one of the industry's most prominent lab heads, not a policy that has been adopted — no government agency or rival lab has committed to it, and Hassabis's own framework treats the mandatory phase as conditional on the voluntary one succeeding first. It lands alongside separate government-led efforts (UK AI Security Institute, NIST, OECD.AI) already doing frontier-model evaluation work, so the practical question is whether Hassabis's industry-anchored body would complement or compete with those.
