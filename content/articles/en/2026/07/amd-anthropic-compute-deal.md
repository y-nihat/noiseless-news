## What happened

AMD and Anthropic announced a strategic partnership on 22 July 2026 under which AMD will make a strategic equity investment of up to $5 billion in Anthropic [1][2][4]. Alongside the investment, the companies plan to deploy up to 2 gigawatts of AMD's Instinct MI450-series (MI455X) GPUs, built into AMD's Helios rack-scale systems, to run Anthropic's Claude models [1][3]. Deployment of the first gigawatt is planned to begin in the first half of 2027 [1].

The deal builds on an existing relationship: Anthropic already runs production workloads on AMD's current-generation Instinct MI355X GPUs, and this announcement expands that footprint rather than starting it from scratch [1][3]. The companies also said Anthropic's Claude models will be used to help optimize AMD's own hardware and software, including its ROCm software stack, and that AMD will adopt Claude internally.

## Why it matters

The investment makes AMD a second major chip supplier for one of the leading AI labs, alongside Nvidia, at a scale — up to 2 gigawatts — comparable to the largest AI compute commitments announced this year. For AMD, it locks in a large committed customer for its Instinct GPU line as it competes for a share of AI datacenter spending long dominated by Nvidia; for Anthropic, it diversifies the hardware supply behind Claude beyond a single vendor.
