## What happened

Amkor Technology and Nvidia announced a multi-year strategic partnership on
23 July 2026 to develop advanced semiconductor packaging and test
technology [1][2][3]. As part of the deal, Nvidia will prepay Amkor $1.5
billion to fund the expansion of advanced packaging and test capacity at
Amkor's campus in Arizona, adding US-based capacity alongside Amkor's
existing manufacturing footprint in Asia [1][2][3].

The partnership covers development of high-density interconnect and
heterogeneous integration packaging — the technology that combines
multiple chip components into a single package — for Nvidia's data-center
GPUs, networking hardware, and other accelerated-computing platforms
[1][3]. Neither company's own materials disclose a specific capacity
figure (such as wafer starts or floor space) or name a proprietary
packaging process; the agreement is described as a roadmap and capacity
commitment rather than confirmation of an already-shipping product line.

## Why it matters

Advanced packaging has become a bottleneck in AI chip supply chains as
demand for data-center GPUs outpaces available capacity at existing
packaging houses, most of which are based in Asia. A $1.5 billion prepaid
commitment to a US packaging site is a concrete step toward diversifying
that supply chain domestically, though the buildout itself will take
years to complete.
