## What happened

xAI announced on 6 July 2026 that it has renamed itself "SpaceXAI," posting the new name and a redesigned logo blending the two companies' branding on X [3][4]. The change marks the final step of a process that began when SpaceX and xAI completed an all-stock merger on 2 February 2026, making xAI a wholly-owned SpaceX subsidiary at a combined valuation of roughly $1.25 trillion — about $1 trillion for SpaceX and $250 billion for xAI at the time [1][2].

The Grok product name and API are unchanged; SpaceXAI is a subsidiary within SpaceX's corporate structure, not a merger of equals [3][4]. X, the social platform formerly known as Twitter, had already merged into xAI in March 2025 and is now an indirect SpaceX subsidiary via SpaceXAI [3][4].

The rebrand follows SpaceX's Nasdaq debut on 12 June 2026, when shares priced at $135 and closed the first trading day at about $161 — a 19% first-day gain that put the company's market capitalization at roughly $2.1 trillion, one of the largest IPOs on record [5]. That figure reflects the stock's first-day close, not a fixed valuation; SpaceX shares have since traded down from that peak.

## Why it matters

The rebrand formalizes, in public branding, a corporate consolidation that puts Grok, X, and SpaceX's rocket and satellite businesses under one holding structure — a scale and scope of vertical integration across AI, social media, and aerospace that has no direct precedent among Musk's competitors.
