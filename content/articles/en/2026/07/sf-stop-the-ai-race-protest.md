## What happened

On Saturday, 11 July 2026, a crowd organized by the group Stop the AI Race marched through San Francisco, starting at OpenAI's Mission Bay office, continuing to Anthropic's office, and ending at Google DeepMind's Embarcadero office, with a detour past venture firm a16z along the way [1][2][3]. The march was led by Michaël Trazzi, a former AI researcher who previously held a hunger strike outside Google DeepMind's London office [1][3].

Outlets that covered the march independently give different crowd counts: SF Standard reported more than 100 people, Mission Local described "hundreds," and the AP wire report cited approximately 200 — with the Daily Californian, alone among outlets checked, putting the figure at about 350 and calling it the largest AI protest in American history, a characterization no other outlet repeated [1][2][3][4]. Taken together, roughly 200 is the best-supported figure.

Protesters carried signs reading "stop slop," "it's not too late to regulate," and "in a race off a cliff no one wins," and chanted for the labs to jointly pause training of new frontier AI models [1][3]. Their stated grievances included AI's contribution to rising rents, job losses, and environmental costs, alongside longer-term safety concerns [1][2]. OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google DeepMind did not respond to requests for comment on the protest [1][3].

## Why it matters

The march is a visible sign of organized public pushback against the pace of frontier AI development, aimed directly at three of the industry's largest labs rather than at government alone. None of the three companies has said anything publicly in response to the specific demand for a joint training pause.
