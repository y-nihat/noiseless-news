## What happened

Anthropic launched Claude for Teachers on 14 July 2026, giving verified K-12
educators in the US free access to premium Claude capabilities, a library of
teaching skills, and a direct connection to curricula mapped to academic
standards in all 50 states [1][2][3]. The product bundles in Claude Code and
Cowork so teachers can build lesson plans from a student's past assessment
and assignment data, and can automate recurring tasks such as reviewing
daily exit tickets [1]. Anthropic says nine education-technology
products integrate with it, including MagicSchool, Brisk Teaching, Canva
Education and Diffit [1].

Sign-up is free for verified US educators through 30 June 2027, which secures
a full year of access; the launch covers individual teachers only, with a
school- or district-wide option described as "coming soon" [1][3]. Anthropic
says conversations from verified teacher accounts are not used to train its
models, and that it worked with the American Federation of Teachers (AFT) on
privacy and safety safeguards [1][3]. AFT president Randi Weingarten called
it "a tool designed by and for educators" [1][2] — a notably supportive line
from a union leader who has separately pushed to restrict AI in the earliest
grades [2]. The product will also be piloted in Detroit Public Schools
Community District, as part of a study on educator well-being and practice
[1][2]. Anthropic frames the launch as one deliverable under its existing
partnership with the Gates Foundation, announced in May 2026 [1].

## Why it matters

Claude for Teachers puts Anthropic directly into a crowded race for US
classrooms alongside OpenAI's ChatGPT for Teachers, Microsoft's Elevate for
Educators, and Google's AI Educator Series [2][3] — even as some large school
districts move to curb student screen time and AI use. The nine named
integration partners and the Teach For America course Anthropic cites are
self-reported by the company and were not independently confirmed by outside
reporting as of publication.
