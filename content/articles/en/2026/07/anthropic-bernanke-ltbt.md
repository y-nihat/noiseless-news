## What happened

Anthropic said on 9 July 2026 that Ben Bernanke — who chaired the U.S.
Federal Reserve from 2006 to 2014 and won the 2022 Nobel Memorial Prize in
Economic Sciences for his work on the 2008 financial crisis — has joined its
Long-Term Benefit Trust (LTBT) [1][2]. The LTBT is an independent oversight
body created to hold Anthropic accountable to its mission; it holds a
special class of stock, Class T shares, that gives trustees the power to
elect a growing share of Anthropic's board over time, with the goal of
reaching majority board control within four years of the trust's formation
[1]. Bernanke joins existing trustees Neil Buddy Shah (chair), Richard
Fontaine, and Mariano-Florentino Cuéllar [1].

Anthropic said Bernanke's appointment draws on his expertise in economics
and how AI is affecting labor markets, along with his experience navigating
the 2008 financial crisis, and that he will contribute to the company's
economic research alongside his broader trustee role [1]. CNBC independently
confirmed the appointment and the trust's governance structure [2].

## Why it matters

The LTBT is the mechanism through which Anthropic's unusual governance
structure is meant to keep commercial pressure from overriding its stated
safety mission, and its trustees' influence over the board is set to grow.
Adding a former central bank chair with economic-crisis experience signals
that Anthropic wants dedicated expertise on AI's macroeconomic and
labor-market effects inside that oversight structure, not just its research
or safety teams.
