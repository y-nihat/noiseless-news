## What happened

Chris Fall resigned as director of the Commerce Department's Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) on 20 July 2026, about three months after taking the job [1][2][3]. CAISI confirmed the departure directly to reporters at multiple outlets; no public reason was given, and a White House spokesperson declined to comment [1][2][3]. Dr. Arvind Raman, who already directs NIST, CAISI's parent agency, will serve as acting CAISI director while Commerce evaluates candidates for a permanent replacement [1][3]. A Commerce official told Axios that Fall's posting had always been meant as a short-term stopgap rather than a lasting placement, though that account has not been independently corroborated by another outlet [3].

Fall is the second CAISI director to leave the post within months. His predecessor, Collin Burns, departed after less than a week on the job in April 2026, reportedly pushed out over his prior work for Anthropic amid the administration's tensions with that company [1]. CAISI itself is a Trump-administration reorganization of the former US AI Safety Institute, created to develop AI testing and evaluation capabilities and support standards for advanced systems.

## Why it matters

Two directors of the federal government's lead AI-testing and standards body have now left within months of each appointment, leaving CAISI under acting leadership for a second time this year as it works on evaluation capabilities meant to underpin US AI policy. Independent confirmation is not yet available on the "stopgap" characterization of Fall's tenure -- it comes from a single anonymous Commerce official -- so that detail is carried here as reported, not established fact.
