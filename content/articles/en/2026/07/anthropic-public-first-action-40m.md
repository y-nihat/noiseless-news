## What happened

Anthropic announced on 21 July 2026 that it has given a second $20 million
donation to Public First Action, a 501(c)(4) group focused on public
education about AI policy, bringing its cumulative giving to the group to
$40 million since a first $20 million pledge in February 2026 [1]. Reuters
wire reporting and The Hill independently confirmed the new donation and
total [2][3].

Anthropic says the donation is driven by findings from its Claude Mythos
Preview model, which it says has discovered thousands of high-severity
software vulnerabilities — including in every major operating system and
browser — through an effort it calls Project Glasswing, aimed at getting
those weaknesses to trusted parties who can fix them [1]. Citing that
capability growth, Anthropic is calling for governments to have the
authority to slow or block deployment of AI models that pose a serious risk
of catastrophic harm [1]. As with the February pledge, Anthropic says the
new funds cannot be used to influence the election of any candidate,
restricting them to public education and policy work rather than direct
campaign spending [1][2].

This follows Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei's personal $1 million donation to
the separate Public First super PAC, disclosed in a campaign-finance filing
made public on 16 July 2026, which first surfaced the February $20 million
pledge to Public First Action as background.

## Why it matters

Anthropic is now the single largest funder of AI-safety-oriented political
spending on record, doubling its commitment to Public First Action in five
months while citing its own model's vulnerability-discovery capability as
the reason for urgency [1]. It puts a concrete dollar figure behind the
company's public argument that AI capability is growing fast enough to
warrant new government authority over deployment — a position rival
AI-industry money, such as the Leading the Future PAC, has organized
directly against.
