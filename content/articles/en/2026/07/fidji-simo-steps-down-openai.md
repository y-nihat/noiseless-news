## What happened

Fidji Simo, OpenAI's second-highest-ranking executive, said on 9 July 2026
that she is stepping down from her full-time role and transitioning to a
part-time advisor position focused on consumer products, advertising, and
health products [1][2][3][5]. She joined OpenAI in May 2025 as CEO of
Applications — a title later referred to in coverage as CEO of AGI Deployment
— reporting directly to CEO Sam Altman [4][1].

Simo said the decision follows a severe flare-up of a chronic illness,
postural orthostatic tachycardia syndrome (POTS), which she has managed since
2019. She went on medical leave in April 2026, and said her recovery is
proving longer and more complex than she had anticipated: "The truth is that
I am only making this decision now because I failed to make it many times
before" [2][3]. Altman responded publicly that he was "really sad about this
and very grateful for all Fidji has done for OpenAI" [3][5].

According to the Wall Street Journal, as relayed by TechCrunch, Simo's
responsibilities will be divided among OpenAI President Greg Brockman, CFO
Sarah Friar, and Chief Strategy Officer Jason Kwon — a detail reported by the
Journal and repeated by other outlets, but not yet independently confirmed by
OpenAI itself or by a second independently-reporting outlet [1].

## Why it matters

Simo's departure removes one of the most senior operating executives outside
OpenAI's research and safety leadership, at a company that has been rapidly
expanding its product and advertising ambitions under her watch. How her
consumer-facing responsibilities are redistributed — and whether OpenAI
confirms the reported three-way split among Brockman, Friar, and Kwon — will
be worth tracking as a signal of where the company's leadership structure is
headed.
