## What happened

Z.AI, the Chinese AI lab behind the GLM model family and listed in Hong Kong as Zhipu AI (2513.HK), has completed and partially activated a data center with roughly 1 gigawatt of capacity that runs entirely on Chinese-made AI chips, with no Nvidia hardware involved, Bloomberg reported on 22 July 2026, citing a person familiar with the matter [1]. South China Morning Post independently reported the same facility, citing its own people familiar with the matter and not citing Bloomberg [2]. Z.AI has not confirmed the report itself and did not respond to a request for comment [1][3]. Neither report names which domestic chip supplier is used in the facility.

## Why it matters

A data center at this scale running exclusively on domestic chips would be a concrete sign that a major Chinese AI lab can build out large training infrastructure without Nvidia hardware, at a time when US export controls have restricted Chinese labs' access to leading-edge Nvidia GPUs. But completing the facility is a capacity claim, not a demonstrated capability one: neither report shows the cluster has yet trained a frontier-scale model, and the chip supplier itself remains unconfirmed [3].
