## What happened

Leading the Future, the super PAC backed by AI-industry investors and executives to support candidates who favor lighter AI regulation, closed the second quarter of 2026 with $31 million in cash on hand for the November midterms [1]. The figure, reported by Axios from the PAC's FEC filing, is independently corroborated by Transformer News's tracker of the same committee's FEC filings, which lists $31.03 million cash on hand as of 30 June 2026 [2].

The PAC had already spent more than $24 million on primary-election races through the end of June, according to CNBC's independent review of the same FEC filings, published a week before the Axios report [3]. Axios also reported that in the second quarter, Leading the Future transferred $20 million to two affiliated groups -- $10 million each to Think Big PAC and American Mission PAC -- a detail found only in that one account so far [1].

The disclosure lands in an ongoing fight between rival AI-industry super PACs over regulation. This site previously reported that Leading the Future spent more than $8 million opposing New York Assemblyman Alex Bores, sponsor of the state's RAISE Act AI-safety law, in a June 2026 Democratic primary that Bores lost; Anthropic-backed Jobs and Democracy PAC spent nearly $7 million on the other side of that race.

## Why it matters

With $31 million still in reserve and tens of millions already spent, Leading the Future has the resources to contest primary and general-election races through November on the side of looser AI regulation, deepening a district-by-district money fight that is increasingly separate from -- and larger than -- traditional Washington lobbying on AI policy.
