## What happened

Etched, a startup building chips specialized for AI inference, closed a
$300 million Series C funding round at a $10.3 billion valuation,
announced 23 July 2026 [1][2]. The round was led by Sequoia Capital, with
Andreessen Horowitz, SK Hynix, Jane Street, and Diffusion Capital also
participating [1][2]. Sequoia and Etched both describe it as the highest
valuation ever for a Sequoia-led Series C [1][2].

The new valuation is roughly double the $5 billion Etched was valued at
in a $500 million round in December 2025, seven months earlier [1].
Etched says the new funds will go toward scaling manufacturing capacity,
including its production in Taiwan and a new facility in Milpitas,
California [2]. The company's total funding to date now exceeds $1
billion [1].

A separate, larger round reportedly in talks — a roughly $20 billion
raise led by Jane Street, first reported by the Wall Street Journal in
mid-July — has not closed and is not part of this $300 million round;
the two should not be conflated.

## Why it matters

Etched is one of a handful of startups betting that chips purpose-built
for AI inference, rather than general-purpose GPUs, can outcompete
incumbents like Nvidia on cost and efficiency for running (not training)
large models. A near-doubling of valuation in seven months signals strong
investor appetite for that bet, even as the company has yet to ship
hardware at meaningful commercial scale.
