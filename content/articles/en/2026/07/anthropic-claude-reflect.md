## What happened

Anthropic launched Reflect on 9 July 2026, a beta dashboard, accessible from Settings on Claude's web and desktop apps, that shows users a summary of their own usage: topics discussed, usage patterns, and task types over a selectable window of the past 1, 3, 6, or 12 months [1]. Rather than a raw activity count, it categorizes usage against Anthropic's "4D AI Fluency Framework" — Delegation, Description, Discernment, and Diligence [1][2].

The feature also includes optional quiet-hours settings, scheduled break nudges, and reflective prompts, such as "What's one thing you want to keep doing yourself, even if Claude could do it faster?" [1][2]. It's in beta for Free, Pro, and Max plan users who have Memory enabled; it doesn't yet cover Cowork conversations, and Anthropic says a "time spent using Claude" view is planned but not yet live [1][2].

Anthropic states that Reflect excludes incognito chats, files from connected tools, and any conversation linked to a health integration, and that the usage data behind the dashboard isn't used for any other purpose [1]. That's Anthropic's own account of its data handling; we found no independent audit confirming it, so it's presented here as a vendor statement rather than a verified fact.

TechCrunch and The Verge both covered the launch the same day, describing the same feature set as Anthropic's own announcement; neither added independently sourced facts or named user/critic reactions beyond their own editorial framing of the feature as encouraging continued Claude use.

## Why it matters

Reflect is part of a broader pattern among AI chatbot makers of building in usage-transparency tools, and several outlets likened it to annual "wrapped"-style recaps — though Anthropic frames it as an ongoing reflection tool, with quiet hours and break nudges, rather than a one-time year-end summary. Because the privacy exclusions are described only in Anthropic's own post, readers should treat those data-handling assurances as the company's claim until independently verified, separate from the confirmed facts about what the dashboard shows and who can access it.
