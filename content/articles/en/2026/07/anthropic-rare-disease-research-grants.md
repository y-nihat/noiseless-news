## What happened

Anthropic opened applications on 20 July 2026 for AI for Science rare disease
research grants, offering successful applicants up to $50,000 in Claude
credits over six months, plus access to Claude Opus and other approved models
for biology research [1]. The program runs two eligibility tracks: one for
clinical researchers, patient organizations, and data scientists doing basic
research on rare genetic diseases, and one for biotechnologists and
early-stage biotech companies working to accelerate drug development for rare
diseases [1]. Applications close 2 August 2026 at 11:59 PM PST [1]. Anthropic
names the Monarch Initiative, Every Cure, the Centre for Population Genomics,
and the Violet Research Institute as organizations already using its AI for
Science program [1].

## Why it matters

Rare diseases collectively affect hundreds of millions of people worldwide but
individually attract little commercial research investment, since each
condition has too small a patient population to justify typical drug-pipeline
economics. By offering compute rather than cash, Anthropic is positioning
Claude as research infrastructure for exactly the kind of under-resourced
science that struggles to attract funding through conventional grant channels.
