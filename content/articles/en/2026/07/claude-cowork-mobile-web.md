## What happened

Anthropic is expanding Claude Cowork — until now a desktop-only agent — to mobile
and the web, in a beta that reaches Max subscribers first and other plans later
[1][2][3]. The launch turns Cowork into a cross-device platform: a task started on
a laptop can keep running in the cloud after the laptop closes, and can be reviewed
or steered from a phone. Anthropic says work can also be scheduled to execute
autonomously at a set time [2][4].

Alongside the launch, Anthropic published usage data from 1.2 million anonymized
Cowork sessions sampled between 11 and 31 May across more than 600,000 organizations.
Software development accounted for 8.7% of use; the largest category was
business-process work such as assembling reports and reconciling spreadsheets
(33.4%), followed by content creation and copywriting (16.4%) [2].

## Why it matters

Coding agents have been the proving ground for autonomous AI work, but the usage
numbers — Anthropic's own, it should be noted — suggest the tool's audience has
already shifted to general knowledge work. The mobile release follows that logic:
office work doesn't end at a desk, and an agent that keeps executing after the
laptop lid closes competes on a different axis than a faster chatbot. The
cloud-continuation behavior itself remains a vendor description until independent
hands-on reports appear.
