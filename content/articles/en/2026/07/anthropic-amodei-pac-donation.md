## What happened

A campaign finance filing made public on 16 July 2026 showed that Anthropic
CEO Dario Amodei personally donated $1 million in May to Public First, a
super PAC that supports candidates favoring stronger regulation of AI
[1][2][5]. It is described as Amodei's first seven-figure political
donation. Five other Anthropic employees donated a combined total of more
than $2 million to the same PAC over the same period [1][2].

The personal donations come on top of a separate, earlier commitment: in
February 2026, Anthropic the company pledged $20 million to Public First
Action, a related but distinct 501(c)(4) group that the company describes
as focused on public education about AI policy rather than direct political
spending [3].

The donations land amid a broader fight between rival super PACs over AI
regulation. Leading the Future, a PAC that favors lighter-touch regulation,
spent more than $8 million opposing New York Assemblyman Alex Bores — sponsor
of the state's RAISE Act AI-safety law — in a June 2026 Democratic primary
for New York's 12th congressional district. Anthropic backed a different
PAC, Jobs and Democracy PAC, which spent nearly $7 million supporting Bores.
Bores lost the primary to Micah Lasher [4].

## Why it matters

Personal checks from an AI lab's CEO and staff put Anthropic's leadership
directly inside the same super PAC arms race it has otherwise funded at
arm's length through "public education" spending [1][2][3]. With Leading
the Future and Public First now facing off in primary after primary, the
Bores-Lasher race shows AI-regulation politics is no longer confined to
Washington lobbying — it is being fought, and funded, district by district
ahead of the 2026 midterms [4].
